  • Rubber Plant

Rubber plants (Ficus elastica) are popular indoor houseplants known for their attractive, glossy leaves and low-maintenance care requirements. Here are some general guidelines to help you take care of your rubber plant:

Light

• Rubber plants prefer bright, indirect light but can tolerate some direct sunlight. Avoid exposing them to harsh, intense sunlight for extended periods, as this can scorch the leaves.

• If your rubber plant is in low-light conditions, it may still survive, but growth may slow down.

Watering

• Allow the top inch of the soil to dry out before watering. Overwatering can lead to root rot, so it's crucial not to let the plant sit in waterlogged soil.

• Water less during the winter when the plant is in a dormant phase and doesn't require as much moisture.

Soil

• Use well-draining potting mix that retains some moisture but doesn't become soggy. A mix formulated for tropical plants or a general-purpose potting mix works well.

Temperature and Humidity

• Rubber plants prefer temperatures between 60-75°F (15-24°C). They can tolerate slightly cooler temperatures but are sensitive to drafts.

• Maintain moderate to high humidity if possible, but they can adapt to lower humidity levels.

Fertilization

• Feed your rubber plant with a balanced liquid fertilizer during the growing season (spring and summer) every 2-4 weeks. Reduce or stop fertilizing during the fall and winter when the plant is not actively growing.

Pruning

• Prune your rubber plant to control its size and shape. Trim any yellow or damaged leaves with clean, sharp scissors or pruning shears.

Repotting

• Rubber plants generally prefer to be slightly root-bound, so you don't need to repot frequently. Repot if the roots are becoming too crowded or the plant is outgrowing its container.

Pests and Diseases

• Keep an eye out for pests such as spider mites, scale, or mealybugs. If you notice any infestations, treat them promptly with insecticidal soap or neem oil.

Cleaning

• Wipe the leaves with a damp cloth to remove dust. This helps the plant absorb more light and stay healthier.

Support

• Provide support for tall or leggy rubber plants by staking them to prevent them from tipping over.
