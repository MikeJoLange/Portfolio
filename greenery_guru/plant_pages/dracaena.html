  • Dracaena

Dracaena is a popular and relatively low-maintenance indoor plant known for its attractive foliage. Here are some general care tips for Dracaena:

Light

• Dracaenas prefer bright, indirect light but can tolerate lower light conditions. Avoid direct sunlight, as it can scorch the leaves.

Watering

• Allow the top inch of the soil to dry out before watering. Overwatering can lead to root rot, so it's essential not to let the plant sit in water.

• Water less frequently during the winter months when the plant is in a dormant phase.

Soil

• Use a well-draining potting mix to prevent waterlogging. A mix formulated for tropical plants or a general-purpose potting mix works well.

Temperature and Humidity

• Dracaenas prefer temperatures between 65-80°F (18-27°C) and can tolerate slightly cooler conditions.

• They adapt well to average indoor humidity levels but can benefit from an occasional misting, especially in dry environments.

Fertilization

• Feed your Dracaena during the growing season (spring and summer) with a balanced liquid fertilizer diluted to half strength. Reduce or eliminate fertilization during the fall and winter.

Pruning

• Prune dead or yellowing leaves to maintain the plant's appearance and encourage new growth. Use clean, sharp scissors or pruning shears.

Repotting

• Repot your Dracaena when it outgrows its current container or if you notice the soil isn't draining well. This is typically done every 2-3 years.

Pests and Diseases

• Keep an eye out for pests like spider mites, mealybugs, and scale. If you notice any infestations, treat them promptly with insecticidal soap or neem oil.

Avoiding Drafts

• Dracaenas are sensitive to drafts, so keep them away from doors, windows, and air vents.

Rotation

• Rotate your Dracaena occasionally to ensure even growth and exposure to light on all sides.
