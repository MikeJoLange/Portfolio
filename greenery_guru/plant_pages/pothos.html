  • Pothos

Pothos (Epipremnum aureum) is a popular and easy-to-care-for houseplant known for its attractive trailing vines and heart-shaped leaves. Here are some general guidelines for taking care of a pothos:1

Light

• Pothos plants thrive in moderate to low light conditions, making them suitable for various indoor environments.

• They can tolerate some direct sunlight, but too much can scorch the leaves. Indirect or filtered sunlight is ideal.

Watering

• Allow the top inch of the soil to dry out before watering. Overwatering can lead to root rot.

• Water the pothos thoroughly, and ensure excess water can drain away. Empty the saucer under the pot to prevent waterlogging.

Soil

• Use a well-draining potting mix with a good balance of organic matter. A general-purpose indoor plant mix works well.

Temperature and Humidity

• Pothos prefers temperatures between 65-75°F (18-24°C) and can tolerate slightly cooler conditions.

• They can adapt to average household humidity levels, but they thrive in higher humidity. Mist the leaves occasionally or place a tray of water near the plant to increase humidity.

Fertilization

• Feed your pothos with a balanced, water-soluble fertilizer every 4-6 weeks during the growing season (spring and summer).

• Reduce or stop fertilizing in the fall and winter when the plant's growth slows down.

Pruning

• Trim back long vines to encourage bushier growth and maintain a more compact shape.

• Regular pruning can also help remove any yellow or damaged leaves.

Repotting

• Repot your pothos when it outgrows its current container or the soil becomes depleted. This is typically done every 1-2 years.

Pests and Diseases

• Pothos are generally resistant to pests, but occasionally, they may attract mealybugs or spider mites. Wipe the leaves with a damp cloth to remove pests, and if needed, treat with insecticidal soap.

Propagation

• Pothos is easy to propagate. You can root cuttings in water or directly in soil. Snip a healthy vine just below a node and place it in water or soil until roots develop.

Support

• Pothos is a climbing plant, and it may benefit from some support. You can provide a small trellis or let it trail down from a hanging basket.
