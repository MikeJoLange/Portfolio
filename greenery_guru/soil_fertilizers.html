Potting Soil

All-purpose Potting Mix:

An all-purpose potting mix is suitable for most indoor plants. Look for a well-balanced mix that provides good drainage and aeration. It should typically contain a blend of peat moss, perlite, vermiculite, and organic matter.

Cactus and Succulent Mix:

For plants like cacti and succulents that prefer drier conditions, use a potting mix specifically formulated for these types of plants. These mixes usually have added sand or grit to improve drainage.

Fertilizers

Liquid Fertilizers:

Liquid fertilizers are popular for indoor plants because they are easy to apply and quickly absorbed by the roots. Choose a balanced liquid fertilizer with equal proportions of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium (N-P-K).

Slow-release Granular Fertilizers

Slow-release granular fertilizers provide nutrients to plants gradually over time. They are convenient for indoor plants as they require less frequent application compared to liquid fertilizers.

Organic Fertilizers:

Organic fertilizers are derived from natural sources and are suitable for those who prefer to use environmentally friendly products. Examples include compost, fish emulsion, seaweed extract, and worm castings.

Specialty Fertilizers:

Some indoor plants may have specific nutrient requirements. For example, acid-loving plants like ferns and gardenias may benefit from fertilizers formulated for acid-loving plants.

When using fertilizers, always follow the instructions on the packaging to avoid over-fertilizing, which can harm your plants. Additionally, it's a good practice to periodically flush the soil with water to prevent salt buildup from fertilizers.
